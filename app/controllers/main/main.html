This is a website that I am building in my (very limited) free time. With this, I hope to keep some of my front-end web development skills sharp. I will be using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, AngularJS, and Bootstrap. I have done a lot with Angular before, and it is a pretty sweet tool for whipping together client-side sites quickly. I will attempt to follow best practices while making use of Angular. I have three reasons for this. First, I will be working on this and adding to it over time, so I want to be able to easily pick up where I have left off at. Second, by attempting to figure out and follow best practices, I find that I often end up learning more about the tools I am using. Third, best practice is best. Finally, I will admit early on that I use Bootstrap for the majority of my styling. I could do it all with my own CSS, but I find that to be really tedious and time-consuming; as a college student, and soon-to-be graduate, I don't have time to do battle with styling.

Update: With my job hunt going and my last semester of classes, I haven't had time to updated this site since winter break. I likely will continue to not have time until I get settled into a job after I graduate. However, I have not forgotten!

My plan for this site is to make it a sort of portfolio. I would like to talk about and show off some of the projects that I have completed through my internships and schoolwork. Unfortunately, tracking down all the projects I have completed may be difficult. My efforts to provide this information on this site will be coordinated with my efforts to populate my GitHub account (Jacoby04) with the projects. I am always hesitant to show off the code for projects I completed as I have built up my Computer Science arsenal over the past few years. The reason for this is a lot of times as projects are completed for school, best practice isn't a concern (or at least not as much as it should be). In other words, some of my code might be down-right cringe-worthy. You gotta start somewhere, right? Consider this a disclaimer, I have improved over the years, and I am always striving to improve further; perfection comes with time.
